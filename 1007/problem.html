Quirky Queries

Bob is painting his wooden fence. Bob has a long fence, so he grabs a can of paint, starts somewhere along the line, and paints wood planks one by one until he runs out of paint, moving in a single direction.

However, he often does this absent-mindedly, and forgets what colour his fence is painted where.

Help him keep track!

Input

The first line of input will contain two positive integers, $n$ and $q$, the number of wooden planks in his fence and the number of queries you will be given. The wooden planks are numbered from $1$ to $n$, and are originally all painted 

white

Then follow $q$ queries, each on their own line
They either represent that he has painted planks $a$ through $b$ (inclusive, $a \leq b$) of his fence with colour 

col
:
p a b col
(
col
will be 10 characters or fewer)
A query can also ask what colour plank $i$ is coloured, in which case you will print the colour:
q i

Output

Output the correct colour for each of the queries of the second type. You do not need to output anything for queries of the first type.

Cases:

20 Points: $n, q \leq 10^3$

40 Points: $n, q \leq 10^4$

60 Points: $n, q \leq 10^5$

80 Points: $n \leq 10^9, q \leq 10^5$

100 Points: $n \leq 10^9, q \leq 10^6$

Sample Input

10 5
q 1
p 2 4 black
p 1 2 green
q 2
q 3

Sample Output

white
green
black
