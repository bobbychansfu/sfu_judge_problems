Dicey Multiples

In the bustling city of Computopolis, there's an annual festival where brilliant minds showcase their logic skills in games of chance and skill. This year, the highlight is a unique dice game called "Dicey Multiples."

You are provided with two standard six-sided dice. Your challenge is to define a function printDiceCombinations(n). This function will take a positive integer $n$ ($1 \leq n \leq 12$) as a parameter and print all possible combinations in which the sum of the numbers rolled on the two dice is a multiple of $n$. Each die has six faces, numbered from 1 to 6.

For each valid combination, print the numbers on the two dice, separated by a space. Each combination should be printed on a new line, and the combinations must be printed in sorted order, first by the number on the first die and then by the number on the second die.

To complete this task, you can the provided sample code and write the printDiceCombinations(n) function. Do not change anything else in the code.
printDiceCombinations(6) prints out: 
1 5
2 4
3 3
4 2
5 1
6 6

Explanation: the first 5 rows results in a total of 6, which is divisible by 6. The last row results in a total of 12, which is also divisible by 6.
