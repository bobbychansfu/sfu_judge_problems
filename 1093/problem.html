Blood Alcohol

In the heart of the city, where the standard blood alcohol concentration limit was 0.08%, Officer Lana's patrol car was equipped with the latest "BAC Detector." This device, programmed with a function known as checkLimit(), could instantly determine if drivers were under or over the legal drinking limit.

One foggy evening, as she neared Duffy's Pub, Lana noticed a car zigzagging across the lanes. She signaled for the driver to pull over and activated her BAC Detector. The device hummed, and in moments, it revealed the driver's alcohol level: 0.16%.

The young driver, a look of regret crossing his features, mumbled, "I thought I had less."
"Sorry, but the law's clear," Lana responded as the device displayed its unyielding verdict: "You are going straight to jail."
Though the streets were quiet, the residents slept soundly, knowing that the checkLimit() function and the officers like Lana were there to keep the roads safe, ensuring that those over 0.15% were taken off the streets.

Write a function named checkLimit() that accepts a single parameter: a floating-point number representing a person's blood alcohol content (BAC) as a percentage. The function should return one of three strings based on the provided BAC:

  • If the BAC is less than 0.08%, the function should print out 'You are free to go'.
  • If the BAC is equal to or greater than 0.08% but less than 0.15%, the function should print out 'You have been charged with DUI'.
  • If the BAC is equal to or greater than 0.15%, the function should print out 'You are going straight to jail'.
To complete this task, you can the provided sample code and write the checkLimit(bac) function. Do not change anything else in the code.
