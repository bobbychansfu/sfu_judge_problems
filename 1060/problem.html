Mr. Milkman

Your mother has sent you to the milkman with a cylindrical bottle. You have to pay the milkman the price for the bottle at a rate of $\$2.35$ per litre of milk. You are given the radius ($r$) and the height ($h$) of the bottle in centimetres.

You can assume the value of $\pi$ as 3.14.

The formula for the volume of a cylinder is:
$V = \pi r^2 h $
Also, $1$ litre = $1000 cm^3$.

Input

Two lines, first line contains an integer $r$, the bottle's radius. The second line contains an integer $h$, the bottle's height in centimeters.

Output

The amount you need to pay to the milkman, accurate up to 2 decimal places.

Constraints

$0 \le r \le 1000$
$0 \le h \le 1000$

Sample Input

8
24

Sample Output

11.33
