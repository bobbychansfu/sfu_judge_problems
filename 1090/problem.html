BackwardsTown

In the heart of the whimsical world of Backwardstown, where life unfurled in a unique reverse, young math enthusiast Ellie discovered a curious parallel between her town's backward ways and the properties of numbers. She devised a function, 'reversedInteger,' capable of turning any integer—negative, zero, or positive—tail-to-head, much like the daily life in her town. This function meticulously mirrored the town's essence, right down to keeping the '-' sign of negative numbers at the forefront, proving that even in a number's reverse, its core identity held steadfast.

Inspired by the story of Ellie from Backwardstown, where everything is uniquely reversed, your task is to recreate her 'reversedInteger' function.

The function should meet the following criteria:

  • It must be named 'reversedInteger'.
  • It should take one integer as an argument. The integer can be negative, zero, or positive.
  • It should return an integer that consists of the digits of the input integer but in reverse order.
  • For negative integers, the '-' sign should remain at the beginning.
To complete this task, you can the provided sample code and write the reversedInteger(number) function. Do not change anything else in the code.
reversedInteger("0") returns 0
reversedInteger("123") returns 321
reversedInteger("-14500") returns -541
