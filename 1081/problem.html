Perfect Numbers

In the mystical land of Numeralia, numbers have a life of their own. Among them, there's a special class of numbers that are considered "perfect." A number is deemed perfect if it's a positive integer that equals the sum of its positive divisors, excluding itself. For instance, the number $28$ is perfect because its positive divisors are $1$, $2$, $4$, $7$, and $14$, and their sum is $28$.

Your task, brave coder, is to embark on a quest to find all these perfect numbers hiding between two given numbers.

Input

Two integers, $a$ and $b$ ($1 \leq a \leq b \leq 10^4$) separated by a space. Please note there are no user prompts for this question.

Output

A list of all perfect numbers between $a$ and $b$, inclusive. If there are no perfect numbers in the range, output "-1"

Sample Input 1 

1 30

Sample Output 1 

6 28

Sample Input 2 

7 26

Sample Output 2 

-1
