Trade Wars

Strange times have fallen on the nation of Nacadia. A king of a neighboring nation has recently signed an order on "Trade and Regulation Impeding Free Flow". In retaliation, some neighbors of Nacadia have signed documents like "Tactical Assessment of Redundant Importation & Freight Fees", while others went so far as to implement a "Tyrannical Application of Regressive Import Fee Framework!" The T.A.R.I.F.F.s, as people started calling them, are now in full effect. To make matters worse, the cities of Nacadia have had a falling out as well, and are now imposing taxes on each other as well. Insanity!

As an aid to the minister of trade, you have been tasked with re-organizing the trade routes of the nation. The nation has N cities, and K of them are designated as trade hubs, who actively trade with the outside. You have a list of M potential trade routes, each connecting two cities, and each with a certain toll fee, equal in both directions. To minimize the impact on the economy, you want to build the routes in such a way that the sum of all toll fees is minimized, while ensuring that each city is connected to at least one trade hub.

Input

On the first line, you will be given three integers $N$, $M$, and $K$ ($1$ $\leq$ $N$ $\leq$ $2*10^5$, $1$ $\leq$ $M$ $\leq$ $2*10^5$, $1$ $\leq$ $K$ $\leq$ $N$).

Then, $M$ lines follow, each containing three integers $a$, $b$, and $c$ ($1$ $\leq$ $a$, $b$ $\leq$ $N$, $1$ $\leq$ $c$ $\leq$ $10^9$), indicating that there is a trade route between cities $a$ and $b$ with a toll fee of $c$.

Finally, you will be given $K$ integers $k_1, k_2, \ldots, k_K$ ($1$ $\leq$ $k_i$ $\leq$ $N$), the trade hubs.

Output

Output a single integer, the minimum sum of trade route toll fees.

Scoring

  • Case 1 (20 points): K = 1
  • Case 2 (80 points): no additional constraints
