Blackjack Scoring

In a game of general blackjack, a player receives n cards. The objective is to achieve a score as close to 7n as possible without exceeding it. Here's how the scoring works:

  • Cards numbered 2 through 10 are scored at their face value (i.e., 2 through 10 points).
  • Face cards (Jack, Queen, King), numbered 11 through 13 are each scored as 10 points.
  • The Ace card, numbered 1, can be scored as either 1 or 11 points, whichever results in a better score for the player.

Write a program that:

  1. Asks the user for an integer n representing the number of cards.
  2. Prompts the user to input card values between 1 and 13, a total of $n$ times.
  3. Outputs:
    • "Busted" if the total score of the n cards exceeds 7n.
    • "Blackjack" if the total score of the n cards is exactly 7n.
    • The total score if neither of the above conditions is met.

Blackjack
Explanation: The card valued at 3 is worth 3 points. In this case, the card with a value of 1 is counted as 11, as it benefits the player. Thus, 3 + 11 equals 14, which is 7 times 2. Therefore, it's a blackjack.
