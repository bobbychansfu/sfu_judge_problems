Smart Sort

Alice just designed a really smart computer: a fully parallel computer! She managed to get it partially funded by an anonymous benefactor, and is now building it.

However, her benefactor has recently asked her a question that made her blood run cold:

"What's the use of such a computer?"

Being a theoretical computer scientist, Alice has no clue how to answer. She comes to you for help, and you suggest doing a simple task: sorting a list of numbers.

Alice is stunned! This is a wonderful idea, she's sure that her computer could do it faster than a regular one. However, she's busy building, so tasks you with writing the sorting program.

Interaction

This is an interactive problem. This means that you have to be extra careful with whitespace and formatting. Output no extra newlines, and exit your code the moment you receive a "-1". The judge will start off by sending you a line of input with two integers: $n$, the number of elements of the list, and $m$, the number of batches of parallel instructions you have, including the one used to solve the problem.

You can send queries of two kinds: a set of parallel instructions or a solution

To send a set of $k$ ($k \leq \lfloor n/2 \rfloor$) parallel instructions, output

q k

on a line by itself, then $k$ instructions (each on a line by themselves, numbers and comparators seperated by whitespace): 

a < b
or 
a > b

Once all instructions have been sent as a batch, the judge will respond to all of them on a single line. In order, it will output 0 if the instruction was false, and 1 if correct. If you sent too many instructions, batches, or your formatting was incorrect, a single -1 will be sent, and you must exit your program.

To submit an answer: print 'a', then the sorted list indices on a single line, seperated by whitespace. The judge will either respond with '1', meaning that you are correct, or '-1', meaning that you are wrong. Either way, exit the program.

Cases:

30 Points: $2 \leq n \leq 10, m = 100$

70 Points: $2 \leq n \leq 100, m = 100$

100 Points: $2 \leq n \leq 1000, m = 100$
