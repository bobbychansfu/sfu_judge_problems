Liste Addition

Ms. Liste (pronounced 'liÂ·stee') is a cool teacher who loves teaching third-grade students how to add and subtract numbers. She wants her students to understand the importance of basic arithmetic operations. One day, she gave her students a new math problem. She provided them with two lists of positive numbers and asked them to add the elements in a new list. However, there was a twist to the problem! She wanted her students to break down the sum into individual digits if it turned out to be a two (or more) -digit number. This cool idea has come to be known as "Liste addition".

Your task is to write a program that reads the first two lines of input, which represent two lists of positive numbers separated by commas ','. Then, output the result of the "Liste addition" list separated by commas.

Input

The first line will contain $n$ integers separated by commas. The second line will contain $m$ integers separated by commas. $n$ and $m$ may not necessarily be equal.

Output

Liste addition applied to the two lines of input. The result must be printed with a comma between each element.
