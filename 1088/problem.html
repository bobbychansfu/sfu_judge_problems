Memories

Finally! Amy is back on Earth. She gets out of her the pod and, surpirisingly uneventfully, gets back home.

But what is this? She hears an annoyingly familliar sound, increasing in volume. "Huh, that makes no se...", - Amy's thought was interrupted by her waking up from the alarm. She is back to reality, and her grand escape from an alien spaceship was nothing more than a strange dream. While the events are fresh in memory, she has to note the story (or most impressive part of the story) down. As they say, a picture is worth a thousand words. Following that logic, an animation is worth thousands. Being a peculliar CS student, Amy is much more comfortable with terminal art than any other form of content creation, and she only ever creates on a 80 x 24 Terminal window. As you have guessed, the task of creating this video now lays on your shoulders.

Write a program without input that outputs a video, or cool animation, to said terminal window. There is no need to use TTY, ncurses, or anything like that. You can simply re-print the whole screen with every frame.

Also record your animation and upload it to your preferred cloud storage. Add a link to the video as a comment at the top of your program. Make sure the video is accessible to anyone with the link.

You will receive 10 points for submitting this problem (and having it compile properly if needed), but at the end of the contest, only your latest submission will be judged manually.
You will be marked out of 100 points for video quality, artistry, and creativity.
The video can not take more than 30 seconds to complete, as the judges have only a certain amount of time.
If you have problems submitting or anything, please come ask the judges to view your video at your computer, and they will mark you from there.
