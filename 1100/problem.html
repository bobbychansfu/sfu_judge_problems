Mr. J's Eggs

Mr. J runs a poultry farm and categorizes the eggs produced into four categories based on their weight: damaged eggs (0 grams), small eggs (11 grams), medium eggs (12 grams), and large eggs (greater than 12 grams). The eggs are packed into trays, with each tray holding exactly 6 eggs of the same category.

The selling price for each full tray varies by the egg category:

  • Damaged egg tray: $0
  • Small egg tray (11g each): $8.50
  • Medium egg tray (12g each): $10.25
  • Large egg tray (>12g each): $12.55

Your task is to write a program to calculate the total revenue Mr. J will receive from selling the trays of eggs. The program should consider only full trays of eggs for sale; any incomplete tray by the end of the input should be discarded and not counted towards the revenue.

Input

  • The first line of input will contain a single integer $n$ ($1 \leq n \leq 500$), representing the number of eggs.
  • The second line of input will contain $n$ space-separated integers, each representing the weight of an egg in grams. The weight will be one of the following: 0, 11, 12, or a positive integer greater than 12.

Output

The program should output a single number, the total revenue from selling the full trays of eggs. The output should be formatted as a floating-point number with two decimal places.

Sample Input 1 

18
12 11 0 12 11 12 11 11 12 12 11 0 13 14 0 12 11 11

Sample Output 1 

18.75

Explanation:
From the sample input, we can sort the eggs into trays as follows:

  • For 12g eggs: There are seven 12g eggs, which is enough to fill one tray with six eggs, leaving one 12g egg unsorted. The revenue from the 12g egg tray is $10.25.
  • For 11g eggs: There are eight 11g eggs, which is enough to fill one tray with six eggs, leaving two 11g eggs unsorted. The revenue from the 11g egg tray is $8.50.
  • For eggs greater than 12g: There are two eggs, which are not enough to fill a tray (since a full tray requires six eggs), so these are discarded.
  • For 0g (damaged) eggs: Three damaged eggs are not enough to fill a tray and are valued at $0 anyway.
So, the total revenue from selling the trays of sorted eggs is $10.25 (from one tray of 12g eggs) + $8.50 (from one tray of 11g eggs) = $18.75.
